Environmental Impact

The concepts of open market and international borders have brought great value to everyday life. However, increased consumption and need for immediate products has permanently changed the way we do production. Unsustainable meat production is the biggest factor that contributes to environmental impact and ultimately climate change. The consumer now holds responsibility for their choices and should embrace knowledge and information on the impact of their market behaviour. What is asked for is delivered, at great cost.

A boom in technological development and a shift in populational movement has taken its toll on the use of cheap and ultimately dirty energy. The world has been gifted with the greatest sources of energy, from the sky to the water, energey and power is everywhere waiting to be harnest. A shift to green energy has been recently seen in societies such of Norway. However, a great change in the political mindset is still very much required. Your choices, as a consumer, voter and citizen define the future of energy.

Climate change surpasses the slight inconvenience of the temperature variations. The impact extends massively to numerous communities around the world.


It is our responsibility as citizens and consumers to be informed and demand accountability when it comes to production impact over world's sustainability and human rights.


Deciding to take small steps in your daily live might be the difference that we are all in need for.

How to Get Involved

    Small Changes for a Significant Impact:

  • Start in your own Home - Be conscious when disposing your waste, when taking long showers or when leaving lights on. Consider a meat reduced diet, and choose to buy in season local produce.

  • Join your community - Demand green policies and ones that will have a sustainable impact in the generations to come.

  • Keep informed - Opt to understand the impact of your choices over far distanced communities.
    For instance: Did you know that the avocado trend is preventing communities in Chile from accessing drinkable water?
